Security Education

Learn about common scams and how to protect yourself

Golden Rules

  • Never share your BVN, PIN, or password via text or email
  • Verify payment alerts through your bank's official app or USSD
  • Legitimate scholarships never ask for upfront fees
  • If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is

Fake Scholarship Scams

Scammers target students with fake NELFUND, university, or government scholarship offers. They often request BVN, bank details, or "processing fees" to "claim" the scholarship.

Warning Signs

  • Urgent messages claiming limited spots
  • Requests for BVN or bank account details
  • Links to unofficial websites (check the URL)
  • Asking for "registration" or "processing" fees
  • Apply only through official NELFUND or university portals
  • Never pay to receive a scholarship
  • Verify any scholarship offer by contacting the institution directly

Fake Bank Transfer Alerts

Fraudsters create fake bank alert screenshots to trick vendors into releasing goods or services without actual payment. They often target small businesses and marketplace sellers.

Warning Signs

  • Customer insists on screenshot instead of showing bank app
  • Inconsistent fonts or formatting in the alert
  • Missing or unusual transaction reference
  • Pressure to release goods before "confirming"
  • Always verify through your bank's official app or USSD
  • Check your actual account balance before releasing goods
  • Use our Payment Verification tool to analyze suspicious alerts

Phishing Emails & Messages

Scammers send fake emails or SMS pretending to be banks, government agencies, or trusted companies. They aim to steal your login credentials, personal data, or money.

Warning Signs

  • Urgent language ("Act now! Account suspended!")
  • Generic greetings ("Dear Customer" instead of your name)
  • Links to suspicious or misspelled URLs
  • Requests for sensitive data (BVN, PIN, OTP)
  • Never click links in emails—type the URL directly in your browser
  • Banks never ask for BVN, PIN, or OTP via email or SMS
  • Scan suspicious messages with our Text Scanner before responding

Fake Job Offers

Fake job postings promise high salaries with minimal work. They often request "registration fees," "training costs," or personal documents before the "job" starts.

Warning Signs

  • Job offer without a proper interview
  • Request for upfront payment before "employment"
  • Contact only via WhatsApp or email, no official company address
  • Too-good-to-be-true salary for minimal qualifications
  • Research the company before applying
  • Legitimate employers never ask for payment to hire you
  • Verify job offers through the company's official website

Investment & Forex Scams

Pyramid schemes, fake Forex platforms, and "investment opportunities" promise high returns. They often use social proof and urgency to pressure victims into investing.

Warning Signs

  • Guaranteed returns or "risk-free" investments
  • Pressure to invest quickly
  • Unregistered or unlicensed platforms
  • Difficulty withdrawing your money
  • Check if investment platforms are registered with SEC
  • Be skeptical of guaranteed returns
  • Never invest more than you can afford to lose
